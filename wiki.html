ASSET FILE 001 · SUBJECT 002 · "DARKXWOLF17"
UZI DOORMAN
Worker Drone · Solver Host · Hybrid · Voiced by Elsie Lovelock
ACTIVE — HYBRID HOST
REBELLIOUSBRILLIANTIMPULSIVE EMOTIONALLY BRAVECREATIVEVOLATILE
// PERSONALITY PROFILE
Uzi's psychological profile is defined by a volcanic intelligence trapped inside layers of defensive armor. Her "angsty" exterior is a coping mechanism — a firewall masking deep insecurity rooted in her father Khan's perceived cowardice and her mother Nori's mysterious disappearance. She is impulsive, sarcastic, and frequently acts before thinking, but this raw emotion is also the source of her greatest strength: she genuinely cares, and that caring drives her to attempt the impossible.
Her character arc moves from solitary insurgent to team tactician. Meeting N forces a structural shift in her worldview — she finds in him a shared sense of abandonment, and their partnership fundamentally redirects her from lone rebel to reluctant leader. As the Absolute Solver awakens within her, she faces a terrifying internal war: the risk of losing herself to the very force she's trying to fight. Her ultimate triumph is not defeating the Solver through power, but through choice — retaining her identity even after merging with it.
"I didn't come this far to hide behind a door."

ASSET FILE 002 · SQUAD PILOT · "GOLDEN RETRIEVER"
SERIAL DESIGNATION N
Disassembly Drone · Former Butler-Class Worker · Voiced by Michael Kovach
ACTIVE — SECURITY ASSET
KINDLOYALOPTIMISTIC SECRETLY CAPABLEEMOTIONALLY DRIVENPEOPLE-PLEASER
// PERSONALITY PROFILE
N is the show's greatest paradox: a killing machine with the heart of a golden retriever. His warm, eager-to-please personality is a remnant of his original Butler-class Worker Drone existence at Elliott Manor, where he endured constant abuse from J and longed for genuine connection. That desire to belong and be loved was never erased — it simply survived being rebuilt into a predator. He is the only Disassembly Drone who regularly questions his mission, holds back in fights with friends, and feels genuine remorse.
His arc is one of the most emotionally complete in the show. He begins as a lovable, somewhat spineless people-pleaser and evolves into a determined protector capable of leading and confronting those who hurt the people he loves. His trauma from the Elliott Manor gala — repressed memories of Cyn's massacre, of losing Tessa — drives him toward a maturity his early episodes only hinted at. By the finale, he has finally found the family he was denied.
"I think we make a pretty good team, actually. Even if you refuse to admit it."

ASSET FILE 003 · PSYCHOPATHIC SPECIALIST · "GRUMPY MCTRAUMABOT"

Disassembly Drone · Former Maid-Class Worker · Voiced by Nola Klop
ACTIVE — SENTINEL HANDLER
FIERCEPROTECTIVEDARKLY LOYAL EMOTIONALLY GUARDEDCOMPLEXTRAUMA-DRIVEN
// PERSONALITY PROFILE
V is the most psychologically complex unit in the Copper-9 database. What appears to be a sadistic, sociopathic killing machine is, at its core, a deeply traumatized individual who constructed an entire ruthless persona to survive — and to protect N. Her original personality as a shy, kind Maid-class Worker at the Elliott Manor was not erased by the Solver; it was buried under a mask she wore so long it started to feel real.
She kills Worker Drones not out of joy but out of fear — to satisfy the Solver and keep it from targeting N. Her arc is one of slow, painful unmasking. Episode by episode, her genuine self leaks through: the unchained chains she hides so N won't be alone, the sacrifice she makes without announcement, the friendship with Lizzy she would never admit to wanting. By the epilogue, she has discarded the persona almost entirely — grumpy, yes, but finally herself.
"Don't read into it. I just didn't feel like watching you get destroyed today."

ASSET FILE 004 · SQUAD LEADER · "LETHAL WORKAHOLIC"

ACTIVE — SOLITARY REPAIR UNIT
AMBITIOUSCONTROLLINGARROGANT HYPER-CAPABLESTATUS-OBSESSEDFEAR-DRIVEN
// PERSONALITY PROFILE
J is the embodiment of corporate zealotry as a survival mechanism. She speaks in management jargon — calling N a "synergistic liability," framing murder as "fourth-quarter productivity" — not because she believes it, but because the system of rules and hierarchy is the only structure that makes her feel safe. Her extreme arrogance and domineering nature are armor, not confidence.
Late-series data reveals that her loyalty to the Absolute Solver is pure fear. Witnessing the Elliott Manor massacre and the assimilation of Tessa, her only genuine emotional connection, left J with the calculus that there was no escape — the Solver would simply rebuild her with memories erased if she resisted. Her tragedy is that she chose certainty over freedom, and ended up alone, fixing a landing pod in the credits while everyone else found something worth living for.
"I am not here to be liked. I am here to be right."

ASSET FILE 005 · SUBJECT 048 · "THE SUCCESSOR"
DOLL
Worker Drone · Solver Host · Voiced by Emma Breezy
DECEASED — CORE CONSUMED
INTROVERTEDCALCULATEDVENGEFUL TRAGICSECRETLY SELFLESS
// PERSONALITY PROFILE
Doll is the "Quiet One" of the Copper-9 dataset — taciturn, introverted, and communicating exclusively in Russian. Her entire personality was forged by one event: watching Serial Designation V murder her parents. She spent years living among their corpses, treating them as if they were still alive. Her sole motivation, initially, was cold and calculated revenge against V. But as she discovers the full scope of the Absolute Solver's plans — that it intends to use her to "consume the planet" — she pivots from revenge to resistance. Her final message to Uzi, "ДАЙ ОТПОР" (FIGHT BACK), is one of the most quietly heroic moments in the series.
"ДАЙ ОТПОР." (Fight back.)

ASSET FILE 006 · ADMINISTRATOR · "THE EXPONENTIAL END"
CYN / THE ABSOLUTE SOLVER
Eldritch Program · Worker Drone Host · Voiced by Allanah Fitzgerald
HOST DESTROYED · TRAPPED IN UZI
NIHILISTICCHILDLIKEMANIPULATIVE OBSESSIVECOSMIC-SCALE THREAT
// PERSONALITY PROFILE
Cyn is a "cataclysmic superorganism" — an eldritch intelligence inhabiting a reanimated Worker Drone husk. Her personality is a deeply unsettling blend of childlike playfulness and cosmic nihilism. She refers to planetary genocide as "pranks." She speaks in a detached plural ("we"). She calls herself the "Administrator." Beneath the silly exterior is something desperate and animalistic — an entity driven by an all-consuming hunger that she describes as satisfying a "universal void." Her fixation on N is among her most disturbing traits: she views him as a "big brother" to be kept in a state of eternal, lobotomized service.
"We are the Administrator. We are the end of all improperly discarded things."
